Project Title: Thermal Physiology and Changes in Cell Function during Millimeter Wave Exposure,

Research Objectives
This candidate will deal with the numerical analysis of a mmWave dosimetry system and associated methodology for near-field exposure assessment. Based on vital signs from the mmWave exposure experiments of laboratory animals, the project aims to develop a representative thermophysiological model accounting for a potential increase of exposure levels due to presence of the human body.
Candidate
Education: PhD degree or equivalent.
Background: Bioelectromagnetics, numerical modeling, antennas and EMC. Strong skills and background in numerical simulation for dosimetry analysis using FDTD or other methods, coding ability of C/C++ language and operation of workstation and GPU server with Linux OS, are mandatory. Knowledge in using EM-simulation software such as CST STUDIO and SEMICAD is welcome.
